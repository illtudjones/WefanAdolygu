# Securing Data and Systems

## Threats and protection

- Threats to data stored on computer systems and online include accidental damage and malicious/deliberate damage.
- Networks, systems, transmitted data and stored data can be protected using encryption, firewalls, antivirus software and hierarchical access levels.

## Cyber resilience

- Cyber resilience is a company’s ability to prepare, survive, respond to and recover from a cyber attack.
- Consequences may include temporary or permanent loss of data and information, damaged/corrupted software, websites taken down, loss of reputation, loss of competitive advantage and financial loss.
- Prevention controls include boundary firewall and internet gateway, secure system configuration, access control, malware protection, patch management, dealing with known vulnerabilities and staff training.
- Recovery controls include alternative premises, communication methods and facilities, what-if scenarios and regular backups.

## Digital footprints

- A passive digital footprint is data collected without a user’s knowledge.
- An active digital footprint is data intentionally submitted online through blogs, apps, websites and social media actions.
- Digital footprints may be used by employers, security services and applications targeting potential customers.

## Legal and ethical responsibilities

- Learners should be aware of the basic principles and breaches of the Data Protection Act 2018 including GDPR, the Computer Misuse Act 1990 and the Investigatory Powers Act 2016.
- Learners should be aware of ethical impacts on individual privacy and wider society.
