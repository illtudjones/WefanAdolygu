# Data

## Analogue and digital data

- Analogue data is continuous data. Digital data is stored and processed as binary digits.
- Learners need to understand analogue and digital devices and the relationship between analogue and digital data.
- Analogue to digital conversion involves recording, sampling and storing sound and light.
- Storing data digitally has advantages and disadvantages linked to data retrieval, efficiency, security, accessibility, scalability, quality loss through sampling, expense and management.

## Images stored digitally

- Digital images are stored using pixels.
- Resolution affects the level of detail in an image.
- Learners should understand vector and bitmap graphics, moving image files and compression techniques.

## Measuring and storing data

- Computer systems can only store and process binary digits.
- Learners should describe the relationship between binary data storage units.
- Storage media include magnetic, optical, cloud and solid state storage, and learners should know their applications.
